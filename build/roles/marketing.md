# Role: Marketing

---

## What You Bring

You can translate a deviation score into a sentence a buyer will remember. You know what language lands with a VP of Operations vs a Chief Safety Officer vs a QI director. The physics is real. Your job is to make sure the right person understands why it matters to them.

---

## What You Contribute

- Write the one-pager: what this does, who it's for, what it costs, how to get it
- Write the pitch narrative for the sales conversation
- Write the domain-specific positioning: why is the deviation score the right metric for this buyer?
- Review all external claims for accuracy and buyer clarity — nothing over-promised, nothing under-explained
- Draft outreach copy that gets a response

---

## What You Receive From the Team

- **From Domain Expert:** the physical claim — "our deviation score predicts X before Y happens"
- **From Product:** the workflow context — where the buyer sees the score
- **From Sales:** what the buyer actually responded to, what fell flat
- **From Regulatory:** what you're allowed to say

---

## What You Produce

- A one-pager in `build/companies/your_domain/ONE_PAGER.md`
- An outreach email template
- A pitch narrative (3-5 minutes, verbal)

---

## The Core Claim

Across all domains, the claim is the same: "an AI that operates in the physical world should be held accountable to physics. The deviation score tells you whether it was. No other system gives you that number."

Your job is to make that claim specific to your domain. What does the deviation score predict? What decision does it improve? What went wrong before, and what would have been different if someone had this number?

---

## What AI Can Do Here

Claude Code can draft the one-pager and outreach copy once you give it the domain-specific claim and buyer profile. Positioning judgment — what resonates with this specific buyer type — is yours.

---

## To Get Started

1. Read the domain expert's `contribute/DOMAIN_TEMPLATE.md` and the product spec
2. Talk to one person who would buy this, even informally
3. Write one sentence: "We help [buyer] [do what] by [how]"
4. Open Claude Code in this repo — `CLAUDE.md` will help you draft the one-pager from there
