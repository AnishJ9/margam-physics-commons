# Role: Legal

---

## What You Bring

You can structure agreements that protect the team and don't scare off customers. Pilot agreements, revenue sharing agreements, IP assignment, and licensing terms — the legal scaffold that makes a company real.

---

## What You Contribute

- Draft the pilot agreement template for your domain
- Review the revenue attribution agreement for enforceability
- Handle IP assignment from individual contributors to the entity
- Review the marketplace license terms for your domain's specific requirements
- Flag any liability exposure in the product claims

---

## What You Receive From the Team

- **From Regulatory:** what the product is allowed to do (limits liability exposure)
- **From Finance:** the revenue attribution agreement to review
- **From Product:** the pilot structure to formalize

---

## What You Produce

- A pilot agreement template in `build/companies/your_domain/PILOT_AGREEMENT_TEMPLATE.md`
- A reviewed revenue attribution agreement
- An IP assignment checklist for the team

---

## What Stays at LINCR AI

The frozen ODE core (MargamSim CF2) is proprietary to LINCR AI. The conditioning layer your team trains is yours — you retain ownership, LINCR gets a license to distribute it through the marketplace. See `LICENSE.md` for the full terms.

The legal role owns making sure every contributor has assigned their contribution to the team entity before the layer is registered. A layer registered with unclear IP ownership creates problems downstream.

---

## What AI Can Do Here

Claude Code can draft pilot agreement templates and review revenue agreements once you describe the structure. Legal judgment — what a customer's legal team will push back on, what creates liability exposure in your domain — is yours.

---

## To Get Started

1. Read `LICENSE.md` to understand what LINCR licenses vs what the team owns
2. Read the revenue attribution agreement in `build/COMPANY_TEMPLATE.md`
3. Draft the pilot agreement template for your domain
4. Make sure every contributor has signed an IP assignment before registration
